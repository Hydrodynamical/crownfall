# CROWNFALL — The Rulebook

*A dueling card game for 2 players. One standard 52-card deck. 15–30 minutes.*

> **Bluff with your blood.** Every card you own is a hit point — and even victory costs one.

---

## 1. The Idea

You command a court of 8 **agents** (A, K, Q, J) armed with hidden **augments** (2–10). Your cards are your life: you begin with 26, and every card you lose — in battle, to attrition, to your own spending — goes face up onto your **Wounds pile**. **Your 13th wound is fatal.**

Deploy face down, bluff with stack size, raise at the last second, and win reveals by the smallest margin you dare — because in Crownfall even victory costs blood.

## 2. Setup (2 minutes)

1. **Split the deck.** The 16 face-value cards (A, K, Q, J) are **agents**; the 36 number cards (2–10) are **augments**. Aces are agents worth 14.
2. **Deal agents rank by rank.** Shuffle the four Aces, deal 2 to each player face down. Repeat for Kings, Queens, and Jacks. Each player now holds 8 agents — exactly two of each rank, random suits. They start in your hand, hidden.
3. **Deal augments.** Shuffle all 36 number cards and deal 18 face down to each player. This is your **deck**. Draw 4 into your hand.
4. **Decide first player.** Each player reveals the top card of their deck; whoever shows the **higher number chooses who goes first**. On a tie, shuffle the revealed cards back and reveal again until they differ. Both players then shuffle their revealed card back into their deck.
5. **Lay out your side.** Leave space for your **Front** (up to 3 deployed stacks) and your **Wounds pile** (always face up, always public).

You now possess 26 cards (8 agents + 4 hand + 14 deck). **Your cards never change hands: you always own exactly 26, and your health is 26 minus your wounds.** Your hand is private and has no size limit.

## 3. Wounds (Your Health)

Whenever you take a wound, move **one of your cards, face up, onto your Wounds pile**, from the first available source:

1. the **top of your deck**; if your deck is empty,
2. your **hand** — fan it face down and your **opponent pulls one card blind**; if your hand is empty,
3. your **Front** — you choose the card, but you **must take an augment (from any of your stacks) while any augment remains anywhere on your Front**; only when your Front holds no augments may an agent be taken.

Take multiple wounds **one at a time, re-checking the source order before each card**. Wounds are public.

**The moment you have 13 wounds, you lose** — with one exception: wounds dealt by a battle are checked only after the battle fully resolves (see §8). Dead cards never return — except number cards healed by Hearts. **Agents never leave the Wounds pile.**

## 4. Turn Order

On your turn:

1. **Draw 1** from your deck. This is mandatory. If your deck is empty, **Bleed** instead: take **1 wound the first time you Bleed this game, and 2 wounds every Bleed after that**.
2. Take **up to 2 actions**, in any combination (repeats allowed):

| Action | What it does |
|---|---|
| **MUSTER** | Play one agent from your hand **face down** as a new stack on your Front (max 3 stacks). |
| **ARM** | Slide one augment from your hand **face down** under one of your stacks, never exceeding that agent's slots. |
| **STRIKE** | Attack the enemy **player** with one of your stacks. |
| **CHALLENGE** | Attack one specific enemy **stack** with one of your stacks. |
| **RECALL** | Return one of your stacks, with all its cards, to your hand. |

Each of your stacks may Strike *or* Challenge **at most once during your own turn**; blocking on the opponent's turn is always allowed and never limited. A stack may attack the turn it is mustered.

**First-turn handicap:** on the game's very first turn, the first player **draws normally** but takes **only 1 action** and may not Strike or Challenge.

**Handling:** stacks sit face down with the **agent on top** and augments beneath in the order armed; keep the cards offset enough that **card counts are always visible**. You may always look at the face-down cards in **your own Front stacks and your own hand — never your deck**.

## 5. Agents: The Rank Table

| Rank | Value | Augment slots | True ceiling* | Role |
|------|-------|---------------|---------------|------|
| **A** | 14 | **0** | 14 | The lone blade — beats any lone K, Q, or J (ties another Ace), costs 1 card to field, never raises, survives ties |
| **K** | 13 | 1 | 25 | The bodyguard — efficient mid-weight, bearer of the Decree |
| **Q** | 12 | 2 | 35 | The tactician — flexible threat |
| **J** | 11 | 3 | 44 | The warlord — biggest ceiling, biggest funeral |

\* Best on-suit augments plus Loyalty: J♠+10♠+9♠+8♠ = 11+12+11+10 = **44**; Q♠+10♠+9♠ = **35**; K♠+10♠ = **25**.

Because slots are strict and stacks are built face down, **stack size is public information**: a 4-card stack must be a Jack; 3 cards is a Queen or Jack; 2 cards is K, Q, or J; a 1-card stack could be anything — including a 14.

**Legality:** every stack is led by exactly one agent and never holds more augments than its slots (raised cards included). **If a reveal ever shows an illegal stack** — led by anything but an agent, or over its slots — **that stack automatically loses the battle**: it goes entirely to its owner's Wounds, and the opposing stack returns to its owner's hand spending nothing, its suit power triggering as a win. If **both** revealed stacks are illegal, both go entirely to their owners' Wounds as in a tie, and no suit powers trigger. Deployment mistakes noticed **before** any reveal may be freely taken back.

## 6. Strike and Challenge

**STRIKE (attack the player).** Point at one of your stacks. The defender chooses:

- **Block** with any one of their Front stacks → **Battle** (§8); or
- **Take the hit**: the defender takes wounds equal to the **number of cards in the striking stack**. The striking stack is **not revealed** and **stays on the Front** — exposed to Challenge until it fights, retreats, or is Recalled.

A defender with no Front stacks cannot block and must take the hit.

**CHALLENGE (attack a stack).** Point at one of your stacks and one enemy stack. The defender chooses:

- **Battle** with the challenged stack; or
- **Retreat**: the challenged stack returns, unrevealed, to its owner's hand — but first it **pays one of its augments (owner's choice) face up to its owner's Wounds**. A lone agent retreats free. The challenger stays deployed.

**KING'S DECREE:** when you declare a Challenge with a King-led stack, you may **flip the King face up** (its augment stays hidden). The defender **may not Retreat** — the battle happens.

Challenge is how you punish bluff walls and half-built monsters; Strike is how you cash bluffs in.

## 7. The Raise

The moment a battle is locked in — a Strike is blocked, or a Challenge is accepted or forced by Decree — there is one raise round **before the reveal**: **starting with the defender**, each player may slide **up to one augment** from hand, face down, under their battling stack. Slot limits apply (a lone Ace can never raise; a full King cannot). Raised cards are fully part of the stack: they count in the total, die with a loser, and can be spent by a winner.

## 8. Battle

1. **Reveal** both stacks completely.
2. **Total** each stack: **agent value + augment pips + Loyalty (+2 for each augment sharing its agent's suit)**.
3. **Higher total wins:**
   - The **loser's entire stack** goes face up to the loser's Wounds. Its agent is dead forever.
   - The **winner spends exactly one augment of their choice** from the winning stack to their **own** Wounds (a stack with no augments spends nothing). The winner's **agent and remaining augments return to its owner's hand** — battles exhaust even victors.
   - The winning agent's **suit power** triggers (§9).
4. **Tie:** both stacks are destroyed entirely — everything to their owners' Wounds; nothing is spent and no suit powers trigger. **Exception: an Ace never dies in a tie** — it returns to its owner's hand.
5. **Timing law:** all card movements from a battle — the loser's stack, the winner's spend, and the suit power — resolve as **one simultaneous step**. Defeat is checked **only after** the battle fully resolves; a Hearts heal can therefore save you from a 13th wound taken in the same battle. If both players are then at 13+, apply Simultaneous Defeat (§10).

## 9. The Four Courts (Suits)

Universal rule — **Loyalty:** each augment matching its agent's suit counts **+2** in battle.

Suit powers belong to the **agent** leading the stack and trigger only on battle wins (or, for Clubs, on muster):

| Suit | Court | Power |
|---|---|---|
| ♠ | **SPADES — The Legion** (aggression) | When your ♠ agent wins a battle, the loser takes **1 extra wound** (normal wound-source order). |
| ♥ | **HEARTS — The Cloister** (sustain) | When your ♥ agent wins a battle **in which it spent an augment**, you may return **one number card from your Wounds to your hand — any except the augment just spent**. If no such card exists, nothing happens. Agents stay dead. |
| ♦ | **DIAMONDS — The Exchange** (economy) | When your ♦ agent wins a battle, it **spends nothing** — all its augments return to your hand. But the enemy has now seen them. |
| ♣ | **CLUBS — The Veil** (espionage) | The **first time each Club agent is mustered** in the game, you may immediately **peek at one face-down card in one enemy Front stack, named by position** ("bottom card of that 3-stack"). Peeking tells them you played a Club; you may decline, but declining forfeits that agent's peek permanently. If the enemy has no Front stacks, there is nothing to peek. |

## 10. Winning and Losing

You **win immediately** when your opponent:

- has **13 or more wounds**, or
- has **no agents left** in hand or on their Front.

**Simultaneous defeat** (both players meet a defeat condition — 13+ wounds or no agents — after a battle resolves): the player with **fewer wounds wins**; if wounds are equal, the **active player wins** — attacker's advantage breaks late-game staring contests.

**The game must end:** every turn removes a card from your deck or wounds you, Bleed escalates to 2 wounds per turn, and every battle — an Ace-versus-Ace tie excepted — permanently kills at least one of the 16 agents. Two turtles bleed out — and because the first player draws from turn 1 and acts first each round, **the first player bleeds out first**: passivity is always a losing line for whoever moved first.

## 11. Table Law

All counts are public on request: either player may at any time count the cards in any deck, any hand (number of cards, not faces), any stack, or any Wounds pile, and Wounds piles may be inspected card by card. Call an audit whenever you like.

## 12. Edge States (quick reference)

- **Deck empty:** the mandatory draw becomes a Bleed (1 wound first time, 2 thereafter).
- **Hand empty:** you cannot Muster, Arm, or Raise; hand-source wounds skip to your Front.
- **No Front stacks:** you cannot Strike, Challenge, Recall, or block; a Strike against you must be taken.
- **Front full (3 stacks):** no Muster until a stack leaves by battle, retreat, or Recall.
- **Deck and hand empty, Front holds only agents:** wounds take agents.
- **No agents in hand or Front:** you lose immediately, regardless of other cards.

## 13. The Economy at a Glance

| Event | Your cost | Their cost |
|---|---|---|
| Win a battle | 1 augment spent (♦: 0; ♥: swap for an old wound) | their whole stack (♠: +1 wound) |
| Lose a battle with an *n*-card stack | *n* wounds + a dead agent | 1 augment |
| Take an unblocked Strike | wounds = its size | striker stands exposed |
| Retreat from a Challenge | 1 augment (lone agent: free) | — |
| Tie | whole stack (Aces walk away) | whole stack (Aces walk away) |
| Do nothing (deck empty) | 1 wound, then 2/turn | same clock |

## 14. Reading the Table

All information asymmetry is bounded and countable, by design:

- **Stack size → rank range** (4 cards = Jack, guaranteed — raises respect slots).
- **Wounds are face up** — count the dead to know which suits, tens, and agents remain.
- **Retreats leak**: the augment paid to Wounds is public; only lone agents flee clean.
- **Unblocked strikers stay deployed** — unrevealed, but a standing, challengeable threat.
- **Returned winners and raised-then-returned cards are known** until redeployed face down among bluffs.
- **A Decree King confesses itself** — the price of forcing the call.

## 15. Variant: The Court Draft (advanced setup)

After splitting agents from augments, skip the random deal: **Player A takes any one complete suit** (its A, K, Q, J and all nine number cards), **Player B takes two of the remaining three suits, and A takes the last.** Each player thus owns exactly 26 cards — 8 agents and 18 augments, same as the standard game. Shuffle your 18 augments as your deck, draw 4; higher revealed top card chooses who goes first as usual. Every other rule is unchanged.

Recommended after a few games: the six court-pair matchups (Legion+Cloister vs Exchange+Veil, and so on) turn the suits into chosen classes, Loyalty into a plan, and the Wounds piles into perfect-information card-counting.
