CROWNFALL

Bluff with your blood — every card you own is a hit point.

1The whole game in one breath

One ordinary 52-card deck. Two players. Fifteen to thirty minutes.

🫀Your cards are your life. You own 26 cards — deck, hand, and army all at once. Every card you lose is a wound. Your 13th wound is fatal.
🂠Everything is face down. Your opponent can count your cards but never read them. Size is public; strength is a secret — until battle turns the cards.
🗡Even victory costs. There is no mana. Winning a fight burns one of your own cards. Losing burns your whole stack. Waiting burns you alive.

2The cast: your eight agents

Raw power trades against capacity: the Ace is the strongest alone and can never grow; the humble Jack can tower to 44. That trade-off runs the whole game.

3Stacks — and the tell you can't hide

You deploy an agent face down and arm it with face-down augments. Everyone can count the cards in a stack — nobody can see them. Because each rank has strict slots, size leaks rank but never strength:

3 cards

4 cards
Jack, guaranteed

Battle math, the only formula in the game:
total = agent + augment pips + 2 per augment matching the agent's suit
That +2 is called Loyalty — suit-matched steel fights harder.

4Your turn

1. Draw a card. Mandatory. If your deck is empty you Bleed instead: 1 wound the first time, 2 every time after. The clock never stops — hoarding is just losing slowly.

2. Take up to two actions, any mix:

MusterPlay an agent from hand, face down, to your front (max 3 stacks).
ArmTuck an augment from hand, face down, under a stack (respect its slots).
StrikeSend a stack at the enemy player.
ChallengeSend a stack at an enemy stack.
RecallTake one of your stacks back into your hand.

Each stack may attack once per turn. Blocking is always free. (The game's very first turn: 1 action, no attacks.)

5Combat: the strike, the challenge, the raise

STRIKE hits the player.
They block with a stack — or take wounds equal to your stack's size, and you never show a card.
Yes: three pieces of garbage hit for 3 real wounds if nobody calls it.
CHALLENGE duels a stack.
They fight — or retreat it to hand, paying one augment as a toll (a lone agent flees free).
A King may flip face up — the Decree — and forbid retreat.
▼ battle locked ▼
THE RAISE — defender first, each side may slide one hidden augment from hand under its fighter. The last poker move before the truth.
▼
REVEAL. Higher total wins. The loser's whole stack becomes wounds — its agent is dead forever. The winner spends one augment to their own wounds, takes the rest home, and their suit power fires. Ties destroy both stacks (a lone Ace walks away).

6The four courts

Suits are classes. The emblem tells you the power.

♠ SPADES — The Legion

Win a battle → the loser takes one extra wound.
The sword through the spade: every victory cuts twice. The aggro suit.

♥ HEARTS — The Cloister

Win a battle in which you spent an augment → recover a different number card from your wounds to hand.
The cross on the heart: the only court that heals. The attrition suit.

♦ DIAMONDS — The Exchange

Win a battle → spend nothing; every augment returns to your hand.
The gem in the coin: victory is free — but the enemy has seen your treasure. The economy suit.

♣ CLUBS — The Veil

The first time each Club agent is mustered → peek at one face-down enemy card, named by position.
The eye in the club: play with the lights on. The espionage suit.

8Wounds, and how the game ends

Wounds go face up in a public pile — the whole table watches the dead accumulate and counts what's left. When you must take a wound, it comes from the top of your deck; when the deck is gone, your opponent pulls blind from your hand; when even that is empty, your front is stripped.

You win the moment your opponent has 13 wounds — or no agents left. And the game must end: the draw is mandatory, the Bleed escalates, and almost every battle kills an agent forever. Two turtles bleed out. The crown falls on whoever moves best, not whoever waits longest.

9Five traps every new player falls into

Building a tower and admiring it. A 4-card stack shouts "Jack," invites retreats, and is a 4-wound funeral if a lean Queen out-reveals it. Winning by 1 is the aesthetic.
Taking every hit. Chip strikes cost wounds equal to stack size. Around the third unblocked strike, start calling bluffs — executing a bluffer's agent is how you get paid.
Hoarding. Cards in hand feel like health. But your deck is a fuse, and when it burns out the Bleed eats 2 wounds a turn — and blind hand-pulls can execute your Aces.
Ignoring the wounds pile. It's face up for a reason. If three tens are dead and you hold the fourth, you know exactly what no 2-card stack can beat.
Disrespecting the naked Ace. One card. Fourteen points. Kills any lone K, Q, or J, walks away from ties — and can never, ever grow. It teaches you the whole game.
