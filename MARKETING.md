# CROWNFALL — Marketing Copy

## The Spiel (simplified)

**Your cards are your life. Spend them anyway.**

CROWNFALL turns the deck in your junk drawer into a duel of courts. Your Kings, Queens, Jacks, and Aces are agents; your number cards are the hidden steel you arm them with. Everything hits the table **face down** — your opponent can count your cards, but they can't read them. Was that three-card tower a 35-point Queen… or a bluff worth calling?

Here's the knife twist: **there's no mana, no gold, no tokens — you pay for everything in health.** Your 26 cards *are* your life total. Arm a warlord and you've staked life on him. Lose a battle and your whole stack becomes wounds. Win one and it *still* costs you a card — because in Crownfall, even victory bleeds.

Four suits, four ways to fight: the relentless **Legion** (♠), the healing **Cloister** (♥), the wealthy **Exchange** (♦), the all-seeing **Veil** (♣).

Learn it in 5 minutes. Play it in 20. Lose a King you loved, and shuffle up again.

**CROWNFALL. Bluff with your blood.**

Magic: The Gathering's strategy meets poker's bluffing — with the 52-card deck you already own. Your cards are your hit points, and every move spends them. Two players, twenty minutes, zero components to buy.
